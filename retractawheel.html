Retract-A-Wheels

Overview

The project aimed to design a solution for Ms. Paula Redmond, a stroke survivor who struggles with balancing her bike during initial acceleration. The goal was to help her ride independently and confidently without altering her normal biking experience. Our team worked under the guidance of the Design Thinking and Communication Program at Northwestern University.

Role:

Product Designer

Responsibilities:

User Research, Human Interaction Design, Prototyping & Testing

Duration:

September 2022 - December 2022

Research and Discovery

The team conducted extensive research to understand the problem and the user's needs:

Interviews and Observation

We interviewed Ms. Redmond to learn about her specific challenges and observed her cycling to identify key issues.

Background Research

We studied the physiology of cycling, the effects of strokes, and existing solutions for balance issues. This helped us understand the muscles involved in cycling, the impact of strokes on balance, and adaptive bike solutions available in the market.

Ideation

We brainstormed several ideas to address the problem:

Idea Generation

We came up with concepts like retractable training wheels, a boosting bike, and automatic stands.

Ranking

The ideas were ranked based on criteria such as durability, user-friendliness, and effectiveness in helping with acceleration. We then made low-fidelity prototypes of our top 3 ideas and then socialized them with Ms. Redmond to see which one she liked best. The retractable training wheels concept was selected for further development due to its simplicity and effectiveness.

Our Final Design

The final design, Retract-A-Wheels, was developed and documented:

Design Overview

Retract-A-Wheels are training wheels that stabilize the bike during initial acceleration and retract for normal riding. The design includes stability wheels, a bungee cord to keep the wheels in tension, a spring-loaded pin for locking, and a cable release grip for easy disengagement.
